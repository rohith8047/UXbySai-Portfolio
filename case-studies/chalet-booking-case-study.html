01 · Context

The chalet, reimagined for a mobile generation.

Chalets are central to weekend life in Kuwait — but the digital booking experience hadn't kept up. Travellers were forced between WhatsApp broker chats and fragmented listing sites with patchy media, unclear pricing, and little trust. We set out to design a single, trustworthy booking flow built around how families actually plan.

02 · Research

Talking to real travellers, hosts and weekend planners.

A blend of qualitative interviews, contextual inquiries, and competitive audits shaped the direction. We mapped two clear archetypes — the spontaneous weekend planner and the meticulous family organizer — and designed for both extremes.

03 · Synthesis

Trust, clarity, and "show me the place" won every conversation.

Three recurring themes emerged: travellers wanted believable imagery and reviews, transparent total pricing including weekend surcharges, and a way to compare a shortlist side-by-side before committing.

04 · Design

An experience that feels less like booking, more like planning.

The redesigned flow leans on full-bleed photography, transparent pricing breakdowns, side-by-side compare, and a shortlist that can be shared via a single link — meeting users where they already plan together: in group chats.

05 · Outcome

From scattered listings to a single, trustworthy plan.

Concept testing with returning users showed clearer comprehension of total cost, higher confidence in the listings, and a strong preference for the new shortlist-and-share model versus the legacy flow.
